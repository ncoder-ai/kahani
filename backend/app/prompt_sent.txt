Scene 19:  The air tasted of ozone and coolant, the emergency lights casting an eerie green glow along the corridor walls. Behind him, hidden in the maintenance alcove, Marcus remained mercifully silent.

The path descended sharply, twisting into service tunnels marked with warning signs about radiation hazards. Rambo noted the rust and deterioration—evidence that whatever operation had once occupied these spaces was now abandoned, repurposed for something far more sinister. Up ahead, the tunnel opened into a larger chamber where catwalks traversed open space between massive tanks filled with murky liquid. Shadowy figures moved along the opposite walkway, their silhouettes distorted by the fluid reflections.

"Secondary junction compromised," announced a metallic voice over a comm system, distant but audible. "Rerouting defensive assets."

Rambo froze instinctively, watching as several figures broke away from the group and began moving purposefully in his direction. Time compressed as it always did in combat—each second elongating into crystal clarity. He counted five targets, all carrying submachine guns equipped with laser sights whose red beams cut through the gloom like knife slashes. Their movements were synchronized, professional—no doubt part of the same black ops team that had welded the hatch shut earlier.

A sudden noise echoed down the tunnel—Marcus coughing violently in his hiding spot. Every head snapped toward the sound, followed by the unmistakable click of safeties being disengaged. The figures broke into a run, converging on the alcove where Marcus lay helpless. Rambo raised his weapon, calculated angles and trajectories in milliseconds, and took aim at the lead figure. But firing would reveal his position instantly, drawing all five combatants to him while leaving Marcus defenseless.
